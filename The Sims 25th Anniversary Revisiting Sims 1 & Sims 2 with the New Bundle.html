The Sims 25th Anniversary – Sims 1 + Sims 2 Bundle Review

Posted May 7, 2025

The Sims turned 25 this February and to celebrate, EA has finally released a special Sims 1 + Sims 2 bundle that fans have been dreaming of for years. Whether you’re a long-time Simmer or just curious about the franchise’s roots, this bundle offers a time capsule of gaming history. But how well do these classics hold up today? Let’s break it down with the pros and cons.

What’s in the Bundle?

  • The Sims: Complete Collection – Base game + all 7 expansions
  • The Sims 2: Ultimate Collection – Base game + all 8 expansions + 10 stuff packs
  • Bonus content – Soundtracks, concept art, wallpapers, and optimized installers

Pros

  • Two iconic classics: You get the full experience of Sims 1 and Sims 2 in one package, including every expansion.
  • Nostalgia preserved: Classic graphics, music, and gameplay charm that fans grew up with.
  • Playable on modern PCs: Finally updated for Windows 10/11—no workarounds needed.
  • Great value: Tons of content for the price, legally and DRM-free on supported platforms.

Cons

  • Clunky controls: Especially in Sims 1, the UI and navigation feel dated by modern standards.
  • No guidance: These games don’t hold your hand. Expect trial and error.
  • Occasional bugs: While improved, Sims 2 can still have compatibility hiccups or crashes without mods.
  • No Mac support: The bundle is Windows-only for now.

Final Verdict

If you grew up with The Sims or want to experience the magic of its earliest days, this bundle is a fantastic piece of gaming history. While not perfect, it still offers some of the richest simulation gameplay out there.

Final Score: 8.5/10 – A must-play for fans and a great retro experience for newcomers.
